How does it work?

First and Third Parties

  • First party requests
    • Type in the address bar
    • Click on a link
  • Third party requests
    • Made by first parties

Unique identifiers

Methods

  1. Cookies
  2. localStorage
  3. IndexedDB
  4. Flash
  5. Cache
  6. Fingerprinting
When multiple first parties include the same third party in their sites, that 3rd party is able to track your browsing behavior across those sites.
Sometimes sites can act both as first and third parties, and they share cookies across contexts.

    • Try Lightbeam!

    1. Get Firefox: http://getfirefox.com
    2. Go to https://mozilla.org/lightbeam"
    3. Click blue button "Download Lightbeam for Firefox"
    4. Click green button "Add to Firefox"
    5. A pop-up says "Firefox prevented this site (addons.mozilla.org) from asking you to install software on your computer". Click "Allow"
    6. A pop-up says "Install add-ons only from authors whom you trust". Click "Install Now"
    7. Go to the Tools menu and choose "Show Lightbeam"
